SUBHASHCHANDRA BOSE

TUM MUJE KHUN DO !

MAI TUME AAJADI DUNGA !

( INDIAN FRIDOM FIGHTER )

ABOUT

Subhas Chandra Bose was a prominent Indian nationalist leader who played a key role in the Indian independence movement against British rule. He was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, India.

Bose was a brilliant student and obtained a degree in philosophy from the University of Calcutta. However, he was more interested in politics and joined the Indian National Congress in 1921. He soon rose to prominence within the Congress and was elected its president in 1938 and 1939.

Bose disagreed with the Congress' nonviolent approach to achieving independence and formed the Forward Bloc in 1939. He then sought help from Germany and Japan during World War II to overthrow British rule in India. He formed the Indian National Army (INA) in collaboration with the Japanese and fought against the British in Burma and India.

However, Bose's military campaign was unsuccessful, and he died in a plane crash in 1945, under mysterious circumstances.

BIOGRAPHY

  • 1897–1921: Early life

    Subhash Chandra Bose, also known as Netaji, was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Orissa, India. He was one of the most prominent leaders of the Indian independence movement against British rule.

  • 1921–1932: Indian National Congress

    Subhash Chandra Bose was a prominent leader of the Indian independence movement against British rule. He played a significant role in the Indian National Congress and advocated for a more militant approach to the movement. He was elected as the mayor of Calcutta and implemented a number of reforms during his tenure.

  • 1941–1943: Collaboration with Nazi Germany

    During 1941-1943, Subhash Chandra Bose collaborated with Nazi Germany to gain support for India's independence movement. He formed the Indian Legion, a military unit made up of Indian prisoners of war, and established a radio station in Germany to broadcast messages to India.

  • 1943–1945: Japanese-occupied Asia

    In Japan, he formed the Indian National Army, a military force made up of Indian soldiers who had been captured by the Japanese. Bose's collaboration with the Axis powers was controversial, and he faced criticism for his tactics.

  • 18 August 1945: Death

    Subhash Chandra Bose died on 18 August 1945 in a plane crash in Taipei, Taiwan, which is now the Republic of China. He had been traveling to Japan from Bangkok, Thailand, when the plane crashed shortly after takeoff.
